Formula Dictionary

Every equation you might need, and what each one actually does.

Quadratics

Vertex form

f(x) = a(x − h)² + k

Vertex is (h, k). a stretches the parabola and flips it down if negative. Best when you know the vertex.

Factored (intercept) form

f(x) = a(x − p)(x − q)

Roots / x-intercepts are p and q. Best when you can read the zeros off a graph.

Standard form

f(x) = ax² + bx + c

c is the y-intercept. Multiply the other forms out to land here.

Axis of symmetry

x = −b / (2a)

Vertical line through the vertex; the x-coordinate of the vertex.

Solve for a

plug in one known point

Substitute a point that isn't the vertex/root, then solve the resulting equation for a.

Quadratic formula

x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a

Roots of any ax²+bx+c. The √(b²−4ac) is the discriminant — negative means no real roots.

Factoring patterns

Difference of squares

a² − b² = (a − b)(a + b)

Two perfect squares with a minus. Note: a²+b² does not factor over the reals.

GCF first, always

pull out common factors

Before anything else, divide out the greatest common factor of every term.

Factor by grouping

4 terms → group in pairs

Group, factor each pair, then factor out the shared binomial.

Trinomial ax²+bx+c

find factors of a·c that add to b

Split the middle term, then group.

Functions

Transformation template

g(x) = a · f(x − h) + k

h = horizontal shift, k = vertical shift, a = vertical stretch (|a|>1) or shrink, negative a flips over the x-axis.

Composition

(f ∘ g)(x) = f(g(x))

Inside-out: evaluate g first, substitute the whole thing into f.

Inverse

swap x & y, solve for y

f⁻¹ undoes f. Graph is the reflection over the line y = x.

Domain rules

denom ≠ 0 · even root ≥ 0

No division by zero; the inside of √ , ⁴√ , … must be ≥ 0. Cube roots & polynomials are all reals.

Even / Odd

even: f(−x)=f(x) · odd: f(−x)=−f(x)

Even = mirror over y-axis. Odd = 180° rotation about the origin.

Interval notation

[ ] include · ( ) exclude · ∪ join

Open circle on a graph → parenthesis. Filled dot → bracket.

Probability

Conditional probability

P(A | B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

"Given B," your whole world shrinks to B. Divide the overlap by B's total.

Union (OR)

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B)

Add the two, subtract the double-counted overlap.

Tree → total

multiply along branches

Multiply probabilities down a path; add across paths that reach the same outcome.

Geometry & Similarity

Geometric mean (altitude)

altitude = (leg₁ · leg₂) / hypotenuse

Altitude drawn to the hypotenuse of a right triangle. Equivalently h = √(p·q) from the two hypotenuse pieces.

Similar triangles

corresponding sides proportional

Set up a proportion of matching sides and cross-multiply.

Cone volume / surface

V = ⅓πr²h · SA = πr² + πrℓ

ℓ = slant = √(r²+h²). "Total" SA includes the base circle πr².

Regular hexagon area

A = (3√3 / 2) · s²

s = side length. Prism volume = (base area)·height.

Equilateral triangle area

A = (√3 / 4) · s²

All sides s. Altitude of an equilateral triangle is (s√3)/2.

Square in a right triangle

s = (leg₁ · leg₂) / (leg₁ + leg₂)

Square sharing the right-angle corner, sides along the legs.

Right-Triangle Trig

SOH-CAH-TOA

sin=opp/hyp · cos=adj/hyp · tan=opp/adj

Ratios from the reference angle θ. Opposite, adjacent, hypotenuse.

Reciprocal ratios

csc=hyp/opp · sec=hyp/adj · cot=adj/opp

Flip sin, cos, tan respectively.

Inverse trig (find the angle)

θ = tan⁻¹(opp/adj), etc.

Use when you know two sides and want the angle.

Pythagorean theorem

leg₁² + leg₂² = hyp²

Find the missing side, then build any ratio you need.
